Multimedia in culture

"Please do not touch and keep silence."

Media, such as touchscreens, interactive kiosks, and video walls, make the above-mentioned sentence loses its importance because it invites the visitor to actively learn about culture through touch, sound, and image â€” in the most accessible way.

Touchscreens go beyond basic information

Visitors can personalize their experience. They can zoom in, adjust text size, and choose their language. This empowers them to explore at their own pace.

All this makes a visit to a museum, art gallery or science center great, yet very individual experience, which positively affects our impressions, quality and quantity of acquired knowledge, eliminate the previously existing limitations, and thus encourages to visit other cultural places.

Multimedia in museums isn't just for visitors; it inspires designers too. Architects can seamlessly integrate multimedia into exhibits, using materials like rusty steel or wood to match the era or theme. Glass elements blend seamlessly, and tables can be custom-designed to any shape, creating a cohesive and stimulating experience for everyone
